Cultural & Historical Gems

Sri Lanka boasts a rich heritage. Explore ancient ruins, vibrant temples, and captivating stories.

Sigiriya

A magnificent rock fortress, once a royal palace, with stunning views.

Quadrangle Polonnaruwa

the Sacred Quadrangle or Dalada Maluwa (Terrace of the Tooth Relic), is the heart of the ancient city of Polonnaruwa

Anuradhapura

One of Sri Lanka's ancient capitals, with fascinating ruins and archaeological wonders.

Hill Country Retreat

Escape the heat and discover Sri Lanka's cool and scenic hill country, famous for its tea plantations.

Nine Arch Bridge

one of the best examples of colonial-era railway construction in the country.

World's End

The feeling of being on the edge of the world is truly awe-inspiring.

Paradise Beaches

Sri Lanka's stunning coastline offers pristine beaches for swimming, sunbathing, and water activities.

Bentota-beach

Bentota Beach, also known as Ventura Beach, is a beautiful stretch of coastline located on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka.

Nilaveli beach

Unlike some of the more crowded beaches in Sri Lanka, Nilaveli offers a more relaxed and secluded atmosphere.

Hikkaduwa beach

Hikkaduwa Beach is a popular beach with beautiful stretch of golden sand, clear blue waters, and vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life

Encounter Sri Lanka's Wildlife

Embark on a safari and witness Sri Lanka's diverse wildlife in national parks.

Sinharaja

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a national treasure of Sri Lanka. It is a world heritage site and a biodiversity hotspot, located in the south-west lowland wet zone of Sri Lanka.

Yala National

Spot elephants, leopards, bears, and a variety of birdlife in Yala National Park and Udawalawe National Park.

Cascading Waterfalls

Sri Lanka is blessed with numerous waterfalls, some shrouded in ancient legends.

Ramboda Falls

the eleventh tallest waterfall in Sri Lanka.Believed to have provided water to Lord Rama.

Laxapana Falls

The name Laxapana is derived from Sinhala words "Laxa" meaning Hundred thousand and "Pahana" or "Pashana" meaning rock.

Diyaluma Falls

The second-highest waterfall in Sri Lanka.The falls are formed by Punagala Oya, a tributary of Kuda Oya which in turn, is a tributary of Kirindi Oya.
